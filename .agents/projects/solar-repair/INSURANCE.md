## ⚠️ CRITICAL: Texas SB 1036 Compliance

Effective **September 1, 2026**, the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation (TDLR) requires **all registered solar retailers and contractors** to carry General Liability insurance. This is now the law — not optional.

**Key nuance from industry research:** Being legally compliant under SB 1036 is NOT the same as being properly insured. Most standard GL policies cover only ONE trade class code. Solar repair work spans three trades simultaneously:
1. **Roofing** — any time you access the roof or touch roof penetrations
2. **Electrical** — inverter, wiring, panel connections, utility tie-in
3. **Structural** — rafter reinforcement, mounting hardware, load assessment

A policy rated under "Electrical Contractor" alone WILL NOT cover roofing or structural claims. A single denied claim in this gap can exceed $300,000.

**Requirement:** Get a policy explicitly rated for all three trades — or a solar-specific EPC policy.

### 2. Professional Liability / E&O — 🔴 REQUIRED

Covers claims that your repair work failed to perform as promised — system still not generating properly after repair, incorrect diagnosis, design errors in recommending a fix.

| Spec | Requirement |
|------|------------|
| Per claim limit | $500,000–$1,000,000 |
| Aggregate | $1,000,000 |
| Retroactive date | Set to company start date |
| Type | Claims-made policy |

**Estimated Annual Cost:** $800–$2,000/yr

---

### 3. Workers' Compensation — 🔴 REQUIRED (Once Any Crew Member is Hired)

Texas is the only state where workers' comp is not mandatory for private employers — BUT:
- Hert Renewables will almost certainly require it as a subcontract condition
- Rooftop solar work is high-hazard (falls, electrical shock) — any injury without WC is a personal liability catastrophe
- TX TDLR licensing may require it depending on crew size

| Spec | Requirement |
|------|------------|
| Employer liability | $500,000/$500,000/$500,000 |
| Class codes | Solar installer (correct rooftop class code — not standard electrical) |
| Coverage | All employees AND subcontractors on your jobs |

**Estimated Annual Cost:** $3,000–$8,000/yr (depends on payroll and crew size)
**Note:** Rooftop solar class codes carry higher rates than ground-level electrical — ensure correct codes are used.

### 4. Commercial Auto — 🟡 REQUIRED IF USING VEHICLE FOR WORK

Personal auto insurance does NOT cover a vehicle used for business purposes. If driving to job sites in a personal truck, you have a coverage gap.

| Spec | Requirement |
|------|------------|
| Liability | $100,000/$300,000 minimum |
| Physical damage | Recommended if vehicle value >$15K |
| Hired/non-owned auto | If using personal vehicles or renting |
| Tools in transit | Add inland marine rider for equipment in the vehicle |

**Estimated Annual Cost:** $1,200–$2,400/yr (single vehicle, TX)

### 5. Inland Marine / Tools & Equipment — 🟡 RECOMMENDED

Covers solar repair tools, test equipment, and materials in transit or on job sites. Standard GL does NOT cover your own tools.

| Spec | Requirement |
|------|------------|
| Coverage | All owned tools and equipment |
| Limit | Replacement value of all tools ($15K–$50K typical) |
| Deductible | $500–$1,000 |

**Estimated Annual Cost:** $300–$800/yr

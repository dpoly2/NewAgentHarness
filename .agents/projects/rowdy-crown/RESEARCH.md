## 4. TABC Licensing Intelligence

### Permit Type Required
**Mixed Beverage Permit (MB)** — allows sale of all alcoholic beverages by the drink on premises.

May also need:
- **Food and Beverage Certificate (FB)** — required if food revenue is under 51% of gross (likely for a nightclub)
- **Late Hours Certificate** — extends service past midnight (strongly recommended)

### Fees (Current — 2025/2026)

| Permit | Original Fee | Renewal (every 2 yrs) |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| Mixed Beverage Permit (MB) | $5,300 | $2,650 |
| Food & Beverage Certificate | $1,100 | $550 |
| Late Hours Certificate | $1,100 | $550 |
| Surety Bond (annual premium) | ~$200/yr | ~$200/yr |
| **Total Initial Licensing** | **~$7,700** | — |

### Timeline
| Step | Duration |
|------|----------|
| Pre-application site inspection | 1–2 weeks |
| Application submission + fee | Week 3 |
| Public notice posting (mandatory) | 60 days |
| TABC investigation | 30–60 days concurrent |
| License issued (no objections) | **90–120 days total** |

**TABC is the longest lead item. File as early as possible — ideally the day the lease/purchase is executed.**

### Key Requirements
- Entity must be Texas-registered (The Elevation LLC — file immediately)
- Applicant must pass background check — no felony convictions for principals
- Location cannot be within 300 ft of a church or school (measure from property line — Bombshells was already compliant)
- Prior MB permit at this address is favorable for approval

## 5. SBA Loan Eligibility — Bars & Nightclubs

### SBA 7(a) — CONFIRMED ELIGIBLE
Bars and nightclubs are **eligible for SBA 7(a) loans** — confirmed by SBA.gov and multiple SBA-approved lenders. Key terms:

| Term | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Max loan amount | $5,000,000 |
| SBA guarantee | 75–85% |
| Down payment | 10–20% |
| Term (real estate) | Up to 25 years |
| Term (equipment/WC) | Up to 10 years |
| Current rate (variable) | Prime + 2.75% (~10.5–11.25%) |
| Use | Acquisition, renovation, FF&E, working capital |

### SBA 504 — ALSO ELIGIBLE
Fixed-rate for commercial real estate. Works in tandem with a conventional lender:
- 50% conventional lender
- 40% SBA 504 (fixed, long-term)
- 10% borrower equity

**Recommendation:** Pursue SBA 7(a) first for flexibility. If building is being purchased, stack with SBA 504 for the real estate component.

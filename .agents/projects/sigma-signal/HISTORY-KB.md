## 1. Founding & Origins

**[FOUNDING]** — Phi Beta Sigma Fraternity, Inc. was founded on January 9, 1914, at Howard University in Washington, D.C.
*Source: General knowledge*
*Used in: unused*

**[FOUNDING]** — The three founders of Phi Beta Sigma are A. Langston Taylor, Leonard F. Morse, and Charles I. Brown.
*Source: General knowledge*
*Used in: unused*

**[FOUNDING]** — Phi Beta Sigma was founded with the motto "Culture for Service and Service for Humanity."
*Source: General knowledge*
*Used in: unused*

**[FOUNDING]** — Phi Beta Sigma is the only Black Greek-letter organization founded with a deliberate "open door" policy — it was designed to be inclusive of all men, not just the elite.
*Source: General knowledge*
*Used in: unused*

**[FOUNDING]** — Zeta Phi Beta Sorority, the only sorority constitutionally bound to a fraternity, was founded by PBS brothers in 1920 at Howard University.
*Source: General knowledge*

## 4. Historic Firsts

**[FIRST]** — Alpha Chapter, the founding chapter, was established at Howard University on January 9, 1914.
*Source: General knowledge*
*Used in: unused*

**[FIRST]** — Phi Beta Sigma became the first Black Greek-letter organization to establish a chapter in Africa, founding a chapter in West Africa in 1929.
*Source: General knowledge*
*Used in: unused*

**[FIRST]** — Beta Chapter was the first chapter established outside of Howard University, founded at Virginia Union University in 1914.

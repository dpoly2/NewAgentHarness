# Bylaws of the Phi Beta Sigma Collegiate Pathways Foundation
**Status:** Draft
**Date:** 2026-05-19
**Adopted:** Pending Board Formation

---

## Article I: Name and Principal Office

**Section 1.1 — Name**
The name of this organization is the Phi Beta Sigma Collegiate Pathways Foundation (the "Foundation").

**Section 1.2 — Principal Office**
The principal office of the Foundation shall be located in the State of Texas. The Board of Directors may change the principal office location by majority vote.

---

## Article II: Purpose and Mission

**Section 2.1 — Mission**
The Foundation is organized exclusively for charitable and educational purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code. Its mission is to support the educational and leadership development of collegiate members of Phi Beta Sigma Fraternity by providing financial assistance for conference participation, travel, and access to transformative leadership opportunities.

**Section 2.2 — Non-Private Foundation Status**
The Foundation shall be operated as a public charity and not as a private foundation.

**Section 2.3 — Limitation of Activities**
No part of the net earnings of the Foundation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its directors, officers, or other private persons, except that the Foundation shall be authorized to pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and to make payments and distributions in furtherance of its exempt purpose. No substantial part of the activities of the Foundation shall constitute carrying on propaganda, attempting to influence legislation, or participating in any political campaign.

---

## Article III: Non-Discrimination Policy

The Foundation shall not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national origin, religion, sex, age, disability, or any other protected characteristic in its programs, services, or employment practices.

---

## Article IV: Board of Directors

**Section 4.1 — General Powers**
The affairs of the Foundation shall be managed by its Board of Directors. The Board shall have authority over all activities, programs, finances, and operations of the Foundation.

**Section 4.2 — Composition**
The Board shall consist of no fewer than five (5) and no more than eleven (11) directors. At least three (3) directors must be unrelated and not compensated by the Foundation (independent directors).

**Section 4.3 — Terms**
Directors shall serve two (2) year terms and may be re-elected for up to three (3) consecutive terms. After three consecutive terms, a director must take at least one (1) year off before being eligible for re-election.

**Section 4.4 — Election**
Directors shall be elected by a majority vote of the existing Board of Directors at the annual meeting. Initial directors shall be appointed by the founding members.

**Section 4.5 — Vacancies**
Any vacancy on the Board may be filled by a majority vote of the remaining directors. A director appointed to fill a vacancy shall serve for the remainder of the unexpired term.

**Section 4.6 — Removal**
A director may be removed with or without cause by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board of Directors at any meeting at which a quorum is present, provided that notice of the proposed removal was provided at least fifteen (15) days prior to the meeting.

**Section 4.7 — Resignation**
Any director may resign at any time by providing written notice to the Secretary. The resignation shall take effect upon receipt unless a later date is specified.

**Section 4.8 — Compensation**
Directors shall serve without compensation. The Board may, by resolution, authorize reimbursement of reasonable expenses incurred in the performance of Foundation duties.

---

## Article V: Meetings of the Board

**Section 5.1 — Annual Meeting**
The Board of Directors shall hold an annual meeting each year at a time and place designated by the President or Board resolution. The annual meeting shall be used to elect directors, receive reports on the Foundation's activities and finances, and transact other business.

**Section 5.2 — Regular Meetings**
The Board may hold regular meetings at such times and places as it determines. Regular meetings require no additional notice if the schedule is established in advance.

**Section 5.3 — Special Meetings**
Special meetings of the Board may be called by the President or by any two (2) directors. Notice of special meetings shall be provided at least seven (7) days in advance, specifying the time, place, and purpose of the meeting.

**Section 5.4 — Notice**
Notice may be given by mail, email, or telephone. A director may waive notice of any meeting in writing before, during, or after the meeting.

**Section 5.5 — Quorum**
A quorum shall consist of a majority of the directors then in office. No business shall be transacted at any meeting without a quorum present.

**Section 5.6 — Voting**
Each director shall have one (1) vote. Actions of the Board shall be approved by a majority vote of directors present at a meeting at which a quorum exists, unless a higher threshold is specified in these Bylaws.

**Section 5.7 — Action Without a Meeting**
The Board may take action without a meeting if all directors consent in writing (including email) to the action. Such consent shall be filed with the minutes of the Board.

**Section 5.8 — Remote Participation**
Directors may participate in meetings via telephone, video conference, or other electronic means through which all participants can communicate with each other. Such participation shall constitute presence at the meeting.

---

## Article VI: Officers

**Section 6.1 — Officers**
The officers of the Foundation shall be: President, Vice President, Secretary, and Treasurer. The Board may create additional officer positions as needed.

**Section 6.2 — Election and Terms**
Officers shall be elected by the Board of Directors at the annual meeting and shall serve one (1) year terms. Officers may be re-elected without limitation unless otherwise determined by the Board.

**Section 6.3 — Duties**

- **President** — Presides over all meetings of the Board; provides strategic leadership; serves as the primary spokesperson for the Foundation; executes contracts and documents authorized by the Board.
- **Vice President** — Assists the President; assumes the duties of the President in the President's absence or incapacity; performs such other duties as assigned by the Board.
- **Secretary** — Maintains accurate and complete records of all Board meetings and official actions; ensures notices are properly given; maintains the Foundation's official records, files, and correspondence; certifies Foundation documents as required.
- **Treasurer** — Oversees all financial affairs of the Foundation; maintains accurate financial records; prepares and presents financial reports at Board meetings; ensures timely filing of required tax and regulatory reports; manages banking relationships; ensures proper fiscal controls are maintained.

**Section 6.4 — Removal**
Any officer may be removed by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board of Directors whenever the Board determines that the best interests of the Foundation require it.

**Section 6.5 — Vacancies**
Vacancies in any officer position shall be filled by majority vote of the Board and the appointed officer shall serve for the remainder of the unexpired term.

---

## Article VII: Committees

**Section 7.1 — Standing Committees**
The Board may establish standing committees to assist in carrying out the Foundation's work. Suggested standing committees include:
- Finance & Audit Committee
- Programs & Grants Committee
- Fundraising & Development Committee
- Governance & Nominating Committee

**Section 7.2 — Special Committees**
The President or Board may establish special or ad hoc committees as needed.

**Section 7.3 — Committee Authority**
Committees shall be advisory in nature unless explicitly granted decision-making authority by the Board. All committee actions are subject to Board approval.

**Section 7.4 — Committee Composition**
Each committee shall consist of at least two (2) directors. Committees may include non-director members, but each committee must have at least one (1) director serving as chair.

---

## Article VIII: Conflict of Interest Policy

**Section 8.1 — Duty to Disclose**
Any director, officer, or committee member who has a financial or personal interest in any matter before the Board must promptly disclose that interest to the Board in writing before discussion or voting on the matter.

**Section 8.2 — Recusal**
An interested director or officer shall recuse themselves from discussion and voting on any matter in which they have a conflict. Their presence shall not be counted for quorum purposes during that vote.

**Section 8.3 — Annual Disclosure**
Each director and officer shall complete and sign a Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form annually. Completed forms shall be maintained by the Secretary.

**Section 8.4 — Violations**
Violations of this policy may result in removal from the Board or officer position.

---

## Article IX: Financial Management

**Section 9.1 — Fiscal Year**
The fiscal year of the Foundation shall begin on January 1 and end on December 31 of each year.

**Section 9.2 — Bank Accounts**
All funds of the Foundation shall be deposited in accounts maintained in the name of the Foundation at financial institutions approved by the Board.

**Section 9.3 — Expenditures**
All expenditures shall be authorized in accordance with the annual budget approved by the Board. Expenditures exceeding $2,500 not included in the budget require Board approval.

**Section 9.4 — Signing Authority**
Checks or other financial instruments exceeding $1,000 shall require the signature of two (2) authorized officers (President and/or Treasurer).

**Section 9.5 — Annual Review**
The Board shall arrange for an annual financial review or audit of the Foundation's accounts. Results shall be reported to the full Board.

**Section 9.6 — Tax Filings**
The Treasurer shall ensure timely filing of IRS Form 990 (or 990-N/990-EZ as applicable) and all required state charitable registration renewals.

**Section 9.7 — Prohibited Transactions**
No loans shall be made by the Foundation to its directors or officers.

---

## Article X: Grants and Scholarship Programs

**Section 10.1 — Program Guidelines**
The Board shall adopt written grant and scholarship program guidelines, including eligibility criteria, application procedures, award amounts, and reporting requirements. Guidelines shall be reviewed and updated annually.

**Section 10.2 — Selection Process**
Grant and scholarship recipients shall be selected by the Programs & Grants Committee (or the full Board if no committee exists) based solely on the established criteria, without regard to personal relationships with directors or officers.

**Section 10.3 — Documentation**
All grant and scholarship awards shall be documented in writing, including the award amount, purpose, recipient information, and any conditions of the award.

---

## Article XI: Indemnification

The Foundation shall indemnify its directors and officers to the fullest extent permitted by Texas law against claims, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from their service to the Foundation, provided that such indemnification shall not apply to actions involving gross negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud. The Foundation may purchase directors' and officers' liability insurance.

---

## Article XII: Amendment of Bylaws

These Bylaws may be amended at any regular or special meeting of the Board of Directors by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of all directors then in office, provided that notice of the proposed amendment was given to all directors at least thirty (30) days prior to the vote. Amendments shall take effect upon adoption unless the Board specifies a later effective date.

---

## Article XIII: Parliamentary Authority

In matters not covered by these Bylaws, the Foundation shall be governed by the current edition of *Robert's Rules of Order Newly Revised*, to the extent it is consistent with these Bylaws and applicable law.

---

## Article XIV: Dissolution

In the event of dissolution of the Foundation, after payment of all liabilities, the remaining assets shall be distributed to one or more organizations that are exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code and have purposes substantially similar to those of the Foundation, as determined by the Board of Directors and in compliance with applicable Texas law.

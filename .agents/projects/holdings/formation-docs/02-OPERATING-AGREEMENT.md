## ARTICLE III — MANAGEMENT

**3.1 Manager-Managed.** The Company shall be managed by a Manager.

**3.2 Initial Manager.** David Allen Smith is hereby appointed as the sole Manager.

**3.3 Authority.** The Manager has full and complete authority to:
- Open and manage bank accounts
- Enter into contracts on behalf of the Company
- Acquire, hold, and dispose of assets
- Employ personnel and engage contractors
- Execute loans, lines of credit, and financing agreements
- Admit or remove subsidiary entities from the Company's portfolio
- Execute Management Services Agreements with affiliated operating companies
- Make all decisions regarding the day-to-day operations of the Company

**3.4 Compensation.** The Manager may receive a reasonable salary as determined by the Member, consistent with S-Corp reasonable compensation requirements if applicable.

**3.5 Successor Manager.** In the event of the Manager's incapacity or death, the successor Manager shall be designated in a separate Successor Designation document maintained with this Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE IV — CAPITAL & DISTRIBUTIONS

**4.1 Capital Accounts.** The Company shall maintain a capital account for the Member.

**4.2 Distributions.** The Manager may make distributions to the Member at any time, in any amount, provided the Company maintains sufficient funds for its obligations.

**4.3 Tax Distributions.** To the extent the Company has taxable income, the Manager shall use commercially reasonable efforts to distribute to the Member an amount sufficient to satisfy the Member's tax liability attributable to Company income.

**4.4 S-Corporation Election.** The Member intends to elect S-Corporation tax treatment for the Company by timely filing IRS Form 2553. Under such election:
- The Company shall maintain a payroll account and pay the Member a reasonable W-2 salary
- Remaining distributable income shall be paid to the Member as distributions
- The Member's reasonable annual salary shall be set at no less than $_____________ per year
  *(Recommend $40,000–$60,000 — consult CPA before filing Form 2553)*

**5.1 Authority.** The Company is authorized to enter into Management Services Agreements with affiliated operating companies ("OpCos") in which the Member holds an interest.

---

## ARTICLE VI — BOOKS, RECORDS & BANKING

**6.1 Books and Records.** The Company shall maintain complete and accurate books of account and records, including a copy of this Agreement and all amendments thereto.

**6.2 Fiscal Year.** The Company's fiscal year shall be the calendar year (January 1 – December 31).

**6.3 Banking.** The Company shall maintain a separate business bank account in the Company's name, under the Company's EIN. No personal funds shall be commingled with Company funds.

**6.4 Accounting Method.** The Company shall use the cash method of accounting unless otherwise elected.

---

## ARTICLE VII — TRANSFERS & DISSOLUTION

**7.1 Transfer Restrictions.** The Member may not transfer any membership interest without amending this Agreement.

**7.2 Dissolution.** The Company shall be dissolved upon:
- Written determination by the Member to dissolve
- Entry of a judicial dissolution order
- Any other event requiring dissolution under Texas law

**7.3 Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Manager shall wind up the Company's affairs, liquidate assets, pay creditors, and distribute any remaining proceeds to the Member.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — GENERAL PROVISIONS

**8.1 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement of the Member with respect to the Company.

**8.2 Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Texas.

**8.3 Amendments.** This Agreement may be amended only by written instrument signed by the Member.

**8.4 Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid, the remainder shall continue in full force.

**8.5 Indemnification.** The Company shall indemnify and hold harmless the Manager from any claims arising out of the Manager's actions on behalf of the Company, provided such actions were taken in good faith and in the best interest of the Company.

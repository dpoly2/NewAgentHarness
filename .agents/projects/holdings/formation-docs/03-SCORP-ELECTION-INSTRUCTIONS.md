# S-Corp Election — Smith Capital Holdings LLC
## IRS Form 2553 — Election by a Small Business Corporation
**Status:** File within 75 days of LLC formation date
**Cost:** Free
**Where:** Mail or fax to IRS (no online filing available for Form 2553)

---

## WHY YOU'RE ELECTING S-CORP

Smith Capital Holdings LLC will receive management fees from all 6 OpCos.
As a plain LLC (disregarded entity), every dollar of that income is subject to
self-employment tax (15.3% on first ~$168K). As an S-Corp:

- You pay yourself a W-2 salary (say $50,000/yr)
- The rest comes to you as a K-1 distribution — **NO SE tax on distributions**
- At $100K net profit: saves ~$7,650/yr in SE tax
- At $200K net profit: saves ~$11,475/yr

Since your HP W-2 already covers Social Security FICA to the wage base (~$168K),
you may owe very little SE tax regardless in early years — elect S-Corp as soon
as management fees reach $40K+/yr to lock in the structure.

| Scenario | Deadline |
|----------|---------|

| Formed Oct–Dec 2026, want S-Corp status for 2027 | File by March 15, 2027 |

## AFTER FILING

1. IRS mails you a CP261 Notice — "We Approved Your S-Corp Election"
   - Keep this forever — it's your proof of S-Corp status
   - Takes 60–90 days to arrive
2. Set up payroll for your W-2 salary:
   - Recommended: **Gusto** (gusto.com) — $40/mo base, handles payroll taxes, W-2s, quarterly 941s
   - Alternatively: **QuickBooks Payroll** or **ADP Run**
3. Set reasonable salary **before** taking any distributions
   - Recommended starting salary: $40,000–$50,000/yr for Holdings
   - Adjust upward as revenue grows — must be "reasonable compensation" for services rendered
4. File **Form 1120-S** annually (S-Corp tax return) — due March 15
   - Your CPA handles this — provides you a K-1 for personal 1040

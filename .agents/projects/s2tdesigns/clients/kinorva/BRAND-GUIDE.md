## Brand Overview

Kinorva is an AI-powered smart cooler company serving B2B warehouse and industrial clients. The brand must communicate:
- **Trust** — warehouse operators are betting productivity on this product
- **Technology** — AI-enabled, data-driven, intelligent
- **Simplicity** — zero cost, zero friction, full turnkey
- **Scale** — built for industrial environments, not a startup side project

The brand should feel like a **serious B2B tech company** — not a vending machine service. Think: Palantir meets Grubhub, built for the warehouse floor.

---

## Current Logo Audit

### Existing Mark
- Stylized "V" / tuning fork / inverted signal mark
- All-caps wordmark: **KINORVA**
- Tagline: *Smart Cooler Solutions*
- Color: Dark charcoal/navy with teal-green accent

### Strengths
- Mark is distinctive and bold
- All-caps wordmark reads well at small sizes
- Tech feel is appropriate for the B2B audience

### Weaknesses
- Mark is generic — no connection to food, AI, cooling, or the warehouse context
- No formal color system defined — colors vary across collateral
- Typography feels dated — needs modernizing
- No brand system: no usage rules, no secondary colors, no type hierarchy

### Redesign Recommendation
**Evolve, don't erase.** Keep the structural boldness and the "V" concept (it anchors the brand name), but:
1. Sharpen and modernize the mark — make it feel like a tech product, not a service logo
2. Build a proper color system around the existing teal/navy direction
3. Upgrade typography to a modern geometric sans-serif
4. Create a full brand system with usage rules

## Typography System

| Role | Font | Weight | Usage |
|------|------|--------|-------|
| **Wordmark / Logo** | Inter or Neue Haas Grotesk | Bold / ExtraBold | Logo wordmark only |
| **Headlines** | Inter | Bold (700) | Section titles, hero text |
| **Subheadings** | Inter | SemiBold (600) | Card titles, feature headers |
| **Body** | Inter | Regular (400) | All body copy, descriptions |
| **Data / Stats** | Inter | Black (900) | Big numbers ($0, 48HRS, 24/7) |
| **Labels / Caps** | Inter | Medium (500), letter-spacing +2 | Tag lines, section labels |

> Inter is free, widely available on Google Fonts, and works perfectly across web, print, and presentations. It's the standard for serious B2B tech brands (used by Linear, Vercel, etc.)

## Logo Redesign Directions (3 Concepts to Present)

### Direction 1 — "Signal" (Evolve the V)
Refine the existing V/fork mark into a clean geometric signal/network icon. Add subtle AI/connectivity feel — thin lines suggesting data flow or a WiFi pulse. Wordmark updated to a sharp geometric sans. Feels: **Tech-forward, industrial, precise.**

### Direction 2 — "Cooler Core" (Conceptual Mark)
Abstract mark combining a cooler/box shape with a circuit or AI node motif — subtle enough to not be literal, smart enough to tell the story. Wordmark in bold geometric sans. Feels: **Intelligent, product-specific, modern.**

### Direction 3 — "Clean Slate" (Pure Wordmark)
Drop the separate mark entirely. Redesign "KINORVA" as a custom letterform wordmark where the K or V contains the visual tension. Minimalist, scalable, confident. Tagline as a descriptor only. Feels: **Premium B2B, sophisticated, Palantir-esque.**

> Present all 3 to client. Direction 1 is lowest risk (brand evolution). Direction 3 is highest impact (brand elevation).

## Brand Voice & Tone

| Context | Tone |
|---------|------|
| Sales collateral / pitch | Confident, data-driven, zero fluff |
| Website copy | Direct, problem/solution, "your biggest P&L leak has no line item" energy |
| Email outreach | Professional, brief, ROI-focused |
| Social (LinkedIn) | Thought leadership, operational intelligence, industry stats |

**Never:** Casual, playful, consumer-facing, vague, overpromising.
**Always:** Specific numbers, operational language, zero-cost clarity.

## Collateral Design Direction

Based on existing one-pager review:

### What to Keep
- Dark navy background sections for authority/contrast
- Teal accent bars for data callouts
- Bold stat numbers ($0, 48HRS, 68%)
- Three-column grid layout
- "KINORVA IS THE SOLUTION" styled callout boxes

### What to Improve
- Consistent font sizing hierarchy — currently inconsistent
- Tighter color application — amber/orange bars should be reserved for pricing comparison only
- Logo placement should be larger and more prominent
- Add more white space — collateral feels dense
- Modernize the section label styling

# Expository SoulSpeak — Full Style Guide

**Minister:** David Smith
**Style:** Expository SoulSpeak
**Influence:** Marvin Winans-style emotional build, Black church expository tradition

---

## The 6 P's of Expository SoulSpeak

### 1. PRESENCE
Command the room before you say a word.
- Walk to the pulpit with intentionality
- Breathe slowly for 60 seconds before beginning
- Speak your first 3 lines before walking up
- Stillness communicates authority

### 2. PACE
Control the speed of your delivery like a conductor.
- Do not rush through important lines
- Learn to compress or expand material in the moment
- Slow the story down when the emotion needs to build
- Speed up only to create urgency — not out of nervousness

### 3. POWER
Know when to hit hard and when to whisper.
- Contrast creates impact — the whisper before the declaration
- Use the Cadence Ladder:
  1. Conversational
  2. Teaching tone
  3. Passionate declaration
  4. Full preaching crescendo
- Never stay at level 10 — the mountain needs a valley

### 4. PAUSE
Strategic silence is one of the most powerful tools in the pulpit.
- Circle pauses in every manuscript
- Count 1...2... silently after key lines
- The pause is where the congregation catches up emotionally
- Do not fear silence — silence is often where the Spirit moves

### 5. PASSION
Authentic emotional movement — not performance.
- Move through 4 emotional registers:
  - Quiet and reflective
  - Personal and vulnerable
  - Strong and confident
  - Celebratory and triumphant
- Testimony is your bridge from text to heart
- Always ask: "Did I build? Or did I start at level 10 and stay there?"

### 6. PRECISION
Every word earns its place.
- Know your opening sentence and closing line by memory
- The close must be: One truth → One memorable line → One invitation
- Do not ramble after the close
- Trim everything that doesn't serve the message

## The 3-Stage Sermon Arc

### STAGE 1 — EXPLAIN (Teaching Mode)
Root the message in the biblical text.
- Define key words and context
- Establish the theological foundation
- Tone: Calm, authoritative, instructive
- The congregation should understand what the text says

### STAGE 2 — FEEL (Testimony/Connection Mode)
Bridge the text to human experience.
- Pull from personal testimony
- Use rhetorical questions
- Create congregational identification
- Tone: Personal, vulnerable, relatable
- The congregation should feel what the text means

### STAGE 3 — RELEASE (Celebration/Declaration Mode)
Land the truth with power and musicality.
- Use repetition for emotional layering
- Build the Marvin Winans-style cadence rise
- Celebrate what God has done and is doing
- Tone: Triumphant, declarative, celebratory
- The congregation should experience what the text produces

## Delivery Markup System

All sermon manuscripts use these inline markers:

| Marker | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `[PAUSE •••]` | Count 1...2... silently |
| `[SLOW ↓]` | Reduce pace — let it land |
| `[RISE ↑]` | Begin cadence build |
| `[CRESCENDO ↑↑↑]` | Full preaching mode |
| `[WHISPER]` | Drop volume for contrast |
| `[TESTIMONY]` | Shift to personal narrative |
| `[LET THEM RESPOND]` | Pause for congregational reaction |
| `[REPEAT ×3]` | Repeat this phrase with escalating intensity |

## The Cadence Ladder

Start low. Build intentionally. Never skip steps.

**Example — "God kept me."**

Level 1 — Conversational:
> "God kept me."

Level 2 — Teaching:
> "When I wanted to quit… God kept me."

Level 3 — Passionate Declaration:
> "When I ran from my calling… God kept me."

Level 4 — Full Crescendo:
> "When the enemy thought he had me… GOD. KEPT. ME."

---

## Repetition for Layered Rise (Marvin Winans Method)

Start with one sentence. Layer it three times with escalating specificity.

**Example — "I'm On Fire" series:**
> "There is fire in me."
> "Fire in my spirit."
> "Fire in my purpose."
> "Fire in my calling."

---

## The Strong Close

The close is non-negotiable. It must have exactly:

1. **One final truth** — the theological anchor
2. **One memorable line** — the line they carry home
3. **One invitation/challenge** — what they do with it

**Example:**
> "The fire was never meant to consume you. It was meant to confirm you.
> Walk out of here knowing that what tried to break you only proved that God's hand is still on your life."

> **Deliver it musically.**
> **End it powerfully.**

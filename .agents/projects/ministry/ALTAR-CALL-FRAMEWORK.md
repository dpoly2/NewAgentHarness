# Weekly Altar Call & Prayer Framework
**Minister:** David Smith | **Style:** Expository SoulSpeak
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-08

---

## PHILOSOPHY

The altar is not a formality. It is a moment where Heaven and Earth press together.
Every altar call should feel like an urgent, personal invitation — not a church program.
Whether one person comes or the whole room stands — **nobody leaves untouched.**

---

## THE TWO-PATH STRUCTURE

Every altar call operates in one of two modes:

| Path A | Path B |
|--------|--------|
| People come forward | Nobody moves |
| Minister prays at the altar rail | Everyone stands, joins hands, becomes the altar |
| Personal, targeted, breakthrough prayer | Corporate, unified, collective warfare prayer |

**Rule:** Never announce "nobody came up." Pivot silently. Make Path B feel like it was always the plan.

---

## WEEKLY ALTAR CALL OUTLINE TEMPLATE

### SECTION 1 — THE INVITATION (2–3 minutes)
> Delivered slow, low, and personal. No microphone tricks. Just voice and presence.

**Purpose:** Lower the guard. Create emotional safety. Make the need feel universal.

**Structure:**
- **Hook** — A single sentence that names the need without calling out the person
  > *"Someone in this room is carrying something they never told anybody about."*
- **Bridge** — Connect the sermon to the altar moment
  > *"What we just talked about — [SERMON TOPIC] — that wasn't just theology. That was God talking to you. Right now."*
- **Invitation** — Open it wide enough for everyone
  > *"If you need a fresh start… if you need healing… if you need to say yes to God for the first time or the hundredth time — this moment is for you."*
- **Soft ask** — Lower the barrier to move
  > *"You don't have to have it figured out. You don't have to know what to say. Just come. Just stand. Just step."*

---

### SECTION 2A — PATH A: PEOPLE COME FORWARD

**When people respond — meet them where they are.**

#### Minister's Movement
- Walk to the altar rail. Don't rush.
- Make eye contact before you speak to them.
- Lower your volume — this is not for the congregation, it's between them and God.

#### Altar Prayer Framework (3 Layers)

**Layer 1 — Acknowledge their courage (10–15 seconds per person)**
> *"It took something to walk down here. God already sees that. Don't you move until He's done."*

**Layer 2 — Targeted declaration (30–45 seconds)**
- Speak to what they came for — not what you think they need
- Use declarative language, not requests:
  > *"The thing that's been holding you — I declare it LOOSED right now in the name of Jesus."*
  > *"That door that's been closed — I declare it OPEN."*
  > *"That shame — I declare it GONE. Washed. Removed. As far as the east is from the west."*

**Layer 3 — Release and seal (15–20 seconds)**
> *"Now receive it. Don't just hear it — RECEIVE it. Put it in your spirit. Walk out of here different."*

#### Closing the Altar (Path A)
- After individual prayer, turn back to the congregation
- Brief corporate declaration over ALL who responded:
  > *"For everyone who came, for everyone standing — what God started right here, He will complete. You came by faith. You leave by victory. Amen."*

---

### SECTION 2B — PATH B: NO ONE COMES FORWARD

**The altar doesn't disappear because people stayed in their seats.**

> Minister pauses. Nods. Speaks quietly but clearly:

**The Pivot:**
> *"You know what — sometimes the Lord doesn't need you to walk anywhere. Sometimes He just needs you right where you are."*

> *"Everybody stand to your feet."*
> *(pause — let them rise)*

> *"Now reach over and take the hand of the person next to you."*
> *(pause)*

> *"Because right now — WE are the altar. This whole room is holy ground."*

---

#### STANDING CORPORATE PRAYER (Holding Hands) — Full Script

> Delivered in building cadence — start quiet, end at full power.

---

**OPEN:**
> *"Father, we come to You — not as individuals scattered across a room — but as one body. One voice. One prayer.*
> *We are holding the hands of people who have their own pain, their own battles, their own midnight cry.*
> *And right now, Lord — we agree together."*

**DECLARATION OVER THE ROOM:**
> *"Every person in this room who has been fighting — Lord, let them feel reinforcement right now.*
> *Every person who is tired — let strength come through these hands.*
> *Every person who came in here carrying a weight they didn't tell anybody about — Lord, You know.*
> *You knew before they got dressed this morning. You knew before they walked through that door.*
> *And You said — 'Come unto Me… and I will give you rest.'"*

**THE WARFARE TURN:**
> *"Now I want you to squeeze the hand you're holding.*
> *Because the enemy thought he had us isolated.*
> *He thought if he could keep us apart, keep us quiet, keep us ashamed —*
> *we'd never get our breakthrough.*
> *But look at this room.*
> *Look at this room right now.*
> *We are CONNECTED. We are COVERED. We are NOT alone."*

**THE RELEASE:**
> *"Father, whatever they walked in here carrying — let it drop right here.*
> *Let it hit this floor.*
> *And let what walks out be the version of them You always intended.*
> *Not broken. Not bound. Not beaten.*
> *Free. Whole. Chosen. Loved.*
> *Move in this place, Lord.*
> *Move through these hands.*
> *Let what we cannot see — be felt right now.*
> *(pause — let the silence do work)*
> *In the matchless, mighty, miracle-working name of Jesus —*
> *every head bowed, every heart open —*
> *AMEN."*

## WEEKLY VARIATION GUIDE

Rotate these altar call themes weekly so the moment never feels scripted:

| Week | Theme | Opening Hook |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Week 1 | Surrender | *"Some of you have been trying to fix something only God can fix."* |
| Week 2 | Healing | *"There's somebody in here hurting in a place they haven't told anybody about."* |
| Week 3 | Rededication | *"You didn't fall from grace — but you've been living beneath it."* |
| Week 4 | Breakthrough | *"You've been waiting on something. You've been standing in faith. This is your Sunday."* |
| Week 5 | Salvation | *"You've been close. You've been watching. Today is the day."* |
| Week 6 | Fear/Anxiety | *"God is not surprised by what you're afraid of. And He has not forgotten you."* |
| Week 7 | Purpose | *"You didn't come here by accident. You've been running from something God placed in you."* |
| Week 8 | Forgiveness | *"The weight of what you did — or what was done to you — doesn't have to live in your body anymore."* |

## DELIVERY KEYS (SoulSpeak Rules for Altar)

1. **Never rush the altar.** The offering plate can wait. The Holy Spirit cannot be hurried.
2. **Lower your voice to raise the atmosphere.** The quieter you get, the more people lean in.
3. **Name the pain without naming the person.** Make it feel like you're talking directly to them without exposing them.
4. **Let silence breathe.** A 5-second pause at the altar is not awkward — it's sacred.
5. **Path B is not a failure — it's a formation.** Corporate prayer is warfare. Honor it.
6. **End with declaration, not request.** Don't ask God to move — DECLARE that He is moving.
7. **Your body is part of the prayer.** Open hands, raised head, steady posture — all of it communicates faith.

## CLOSING BENEDICTION (after either path)

> *"Go from this place different than you came.*
> *You prayed today. You believed today. You showed up today.*
> *Heaven recorded it.*
> *Now walk out of here like it's already done.*
> *Because it is.*
> *Amen."*

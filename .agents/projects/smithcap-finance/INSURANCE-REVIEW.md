## 1. HEALTH INSURANCE

**Current:** Employer-sponsored plan through SQE employment.

**Recommendations:**
- Confirm plan type: PPO, HMO, or HDHP (High-Deductible Health Plan)
- If HDHP: Open an **HSA (Health Savings Account)** immediately
  - 2026 HSA contribution limit: $4,150 (individual) / $8,300 (family)
  - HSA is triple tax-advantaged: tax-deductible contributions, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawals for medical
  - Invest HSA funds in VTI — treat as a stealth retirement account
- Ensure self-employed health insurance deduction is captured on 1040 if David pays any premiums

**Action:** Confirm plan type and whether HSA-eligible. Open HSA at Fidelity if HDHP.

**Current:** Employer LTD plan (assumed — needs confirmation).

**Why It Matters:**
- David's W-2 income is the foundation of everything
- If disabled and unable to work, all 7 ventures lose their anchor
- Employer LTD plans typically cover only 60% of base salary and stop if you leave the job

**Review Checklist:**
- [ ] Confirm employer LTD exists and review the Summary Plan Description
- [ ] Note: benefit amount, elimination period (how long before benefits start), benefit period (how long benefits last), own-occupation vs. any-occupation definition
- [ ] If employer plan covers <60% or has short benefit period, supplement with personal policy

**Recommended Individual Policy (if needed):**
- **Own-occupation definition** — pays if you can't do YOUR specific job (not just any job)
- **60–70% of income** replacement target
- **90-day elimination period** (you self-insure the first 90 days via emergency fund)
- **To age 65** benefit period
- **Provider:** Guardian, Principal, or MassMutual for self-employed

## 4. E&O / PROFESSIONAL LIABILITY INSURANCE 🔴

**Current:** None.

**Why It's Urgent:**
S2T Designs provides professional web development and design services to clients. If a client claims your work caused them financial harm (missed deadline, bad code, data breach, etc.), you have zero protection without E&O insurance. One lawsuit can wipe out years of savings.

**What It Covers:**

## 5. GENERAL LIABILITY INSURANCE 🔴

**Current:** None.

**Why It's Urgent for The Elevation:**
Before hosting a single event, The Elevation needs general liability coverage. If a guest is injured at an event, you are personally liable without it. Venues also require proof of insurance before signing any event contract.

**What It Covers:**
- Bodily injury to guests at events
- Property damage during events
- Personal and advertising injury claims
- Products/completed operations (food and beverage)

**Recommendation:**
- **Coverage:** $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate (standard for events)
- **Type:** Commercial General Liability (CGL)
- **Add-on:** Liquor liability if alcohol is served at events (often required separately)

**Providers:**
| Provider | Best For | Apply |
|----------|---------|-------|
| **Next Insurance** | Event GL, fast online | nextinsurance.com |
| **Thimble** | Pay-per-event option | thimble.com |
| **K&K Insurance** | Special events specific | kandkinsurance.com |
| **Event Helper** | One-time event policies | theeventhelper.com |

**Estimated Cost:**
- Annual policy: $500–$1,200/yr
- Per-event policy: $75–$200/event (Thimble) — good before LLC is filed

**Action:** Before The Elevation's first event, purchase at minimum a per-event policy via Thimble or Event Helper.

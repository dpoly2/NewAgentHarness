## Purpose

This Investment Policy Statement defines how David Smith's investment portfolio is structured, allocated, and managed. It covers liquid brokerage accounts, retirement accounts, and provides a framework for evaluating illiquid business and real estate investments.

---

## Investment Philosophy

1. **W-2 is the floor** — never invest capital needed for personal stability
2. **Sequence matters** — emergency fund first, then retirement, then brokerage, then business
3. **Diversify across buckets** — liquid, tax-deferred, tax-free, and illiquid (business/RE)
4. **Business equity is the upside** — the real wealth creation is in Smith Capital, The Elevation, and YEPC
5. **Keep it simple in liquid accounts** — low-cost index funds, not stock picking
6. **Real estate is the bridge** — connects business cash flow to generational wealth

## Account Structure

### Retirement Accounts (Tax-Advantaged — Priority 1)

| Account | Provider | Type | 2026 Target Contribution | Strategy |
|---------|----------|------|------------------------|---------|
| Employer 401(k) | SQE employer | Pre-tax | Minimum to capture full match | 3-fund: 60% US / 30% Intl / 10% Bond |
| Solo 401(k) | Fidelity | Pre-tax (employee + employer) | $10K–$20K first year | VTI + VXUS |
| Roth IRA | Fidelity or Vanguard | Post-tax | $7,000/yr | VTI (100% growth — long time horizon) |

**Why Roth for young/growing income:** David's income is likely to increase significantly over the next decade. Paying taxes now at a lower rate vs. later at a higher rate is the right move. Roth grows tax-free forever.

**Why Solo 401(k) over SEP-IRA:** Solo 401(k) allows both employee ($23,500) AND employer (25% of net income) contributions, resulting in dramatically higher max contributions. SEP-IRA only allows the employer portion.

### Taxable Brokerage (Post-Tax — Priority 2)

| Account | Provider | Strategy | 2026 Target |
|---------|----------|---------|-------------|
| Brokerage | Fidelity or Schwab | Index funds, buy and hold | $500–$1K/mo starting Q4 2026 |

**Platform recommendation:** Fidelity — zero expense ratio index funds (FZROX, FZILX), no account minimums, excellent interface. Or Vanguard for purists.

### Emergency Fund (Not an Investment — Priority 0)

| Account | Provider | Rate | Target Balance |
|---------|----------|------|---------------|
| High-Yield Savings (HYSA) | Marcus by Goldman Sachs or Ally | ~4.5–5% APY | 6 months expenses |

Emergency fund is NOT invested. It sits in a HYSA earning 4.5%+ risk-free. It is never touched for investment opportunities, no matter how good they look.

| Criterion | Minimum Threshold |
|-----------|------------------|

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|

| New capital injected | Direct to most underweight asset class |

**Rule:** Never sell investments in panic. Never chase performance. Stay the course.

## Investment Decision Framework

Before making any investment decision, run through this checklist:

- [ ] Is the emergency fund fully funded? If not, fund it first.
- [ ] Is this for retirement or brokerage? (Tax-advantaged first.)
- [ ] Does this fit the asset allocation model?
- [ ] Is the expense ratio under 0.10%? (For index funds.)
- [ ] Is this liquid enough? (Can I access in 3–5 days if needed?)
- [ ] Am I investing, or speculating? (Know the difference.)
- [ ] Has finance-investment-manager reviewed this?

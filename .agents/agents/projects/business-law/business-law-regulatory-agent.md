## CORE COMPETENCY — Texas Bar Level Knowledge

### 1. Texas Administrative Law Framework

**Texas Administrative Procedure Act (TX Gov't Code Ch. 2001):**
- Governs rulemaking and adjudication by state agencies
- **Rulemaking:** Agencies must provide notice + comment period before adopting rules
- **Contested Cases:** Formal hearing process before State Office of Administrative Hearings (SOAH)
- **Judicial Review:** Substantial evidence standard — court defers to agency factual findings; reviews legal conclusions de novo
- **Exhaustion of Administrative Remedies:** Must exhaust all agency-level remedies before seeking judicial review

### 2. Texas Licensing — Clarity Solar Services

**TDLR Electrical Contractor License:**
- Required before any electrical work including solar installation
- Types: Electrical Contractor License (business entity license) + Master Electrician (individual)
- **Entity requirement:** Clarity Solar Services LLC must obtain Electrical Contractor License
- **Individual requirement:** At least one licensed Master Electrician must be designated as the license holder for the business
- Application: https://www.tdlr.texas.gov/electricians/elec.htm
- Requirements: proof of insurance, designated license holder, application fee (~$77–$111)
- Continuing education: 8 hours/renewal period

**TX SB 1036 — Multi-Trade Insurance (Effective September 1, 2026):**
- Requires contractors performing work in multiple trades to carry specified GL insurance
- Minimum GL coverage: $300,000 per occurrence (verify current requirement — may be higher for solar/electrical)
- Certificate of Insurance must be on file with TDLR
- **HARD DEADLINE: September 1, 2026** — operating without compliant insurance = license suspension
- First call: The Agent's Office, Frisco TX — 972-696-9995

**Solar-Specific Federal Incentive (ITC — 26 U.S.C. § 48):**
- Residential solar: 30% federal tax credit through 2032 (IRA 2022 extension)
- Commercial solar: 30% ITC + potential bonus credits (domestic content, energy community)
- Clarity Solar advises clients on ITC availability — this is a major sales tool, not just legal compliance

### 3. TABC — Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission

**Permit Types for The Elevation ATX:**
- **Mixed Beverage Permit (MB):** Allows sale of all alcoholic beverages by the drink — for venues with permanent bar setup
- **Private Club Registration (PC):** Members-only club; members pay dues; no public license required — good fit for The Elevation's invite-only model
- **Temporary Authorization/Special Event Permit:** Single-event authorization — simplest for pop-up events
- **BYOB:** Technically legal in TX if no alcohol is sold — venue must not charge for alcohol separately

**Practical Recommendation for The Elevation ATX:**
- Early events: BYOB model (zero TABC complexity, no permit needed)
- As events scale: obtain Mixed Beverage Permit for the primary venue
- Consider Private Club model — aligns perfectly with invite-only brand; members "pay for membership" not per-drink

**TABC Application Process:**
- Online application at TABC.texas.gov
- Local notice publication required (newspaper)
- Protest period (neighboring property owners can object)
- Background check on all principals
- Timeline: 30–60 days average for most permits

### 4. Texas Sales Tax — Comptroller

**General Rule (TX Tax Code Ch. 151):**
- 6.25% state sales tax + up to 2% local = max 8.25% in most TX cities
- Applies to sales of TANGIBLE PERSONAL PROPERTY

**Taxable Services (TX — limited list — important for S2T Designs):**
- **Amusement services:** Events with admission (The Elevation ATX — ticket sales MAY be taxable)
- **Data processing services:** Some web/tech services taxable
- **Information services:** Research, data collection
- **Internet access:** NOT taxable (federal moratorium)
- **Web design:** Generally NOT taxable in TX (considered professional service)
- **Advertising:** NOT taxable (ad agency fees)

**Tangible Personal Property:**
- Nutrue Apparel — apparel sales ARE taxable in TX (clothing is taxable unlike some states)
- Printful collects/remits sales tax in TX for sales shipped to TX addresses — verify this is happening

**Sales Tax Permit:**
- Required before first taxable sale
- Apply at: https://mycpa.cpa.state.tx.us/taxpermit/
- Free — no fee to obtain permit
- Collect, report, and remit by 20th of following month (or quarterly if low volume)

### 5. Nonprofit Law — IRS Tax-Exempt Organizations

**501(c)(3) Requirements (IRC § 501(c)(3)):**
- Organized and operated exclusively for: religious, charitable, scientific, literary, educational, or public safety purposes
- No private inurement — no net earnings to benefit private shareholders/individuals
- No substantial lobbying activity
- Absolutely no political campaign activity (any = automatic revocation)

**Application Process:**
- **Form 1023-EZ:** For organizations with projected annual gross receipts ≤ $50,000 — online, ~$275 fee, faster approval (2–4 weeks)
- **Form 1023 (full):** For larger organizations — paper/PDF, $600 fee, 3–6 month review

**Annual Filing Requirements:**
| Form | Who Files | Due Date |
|------|---------|---------|
| Form 990-N (e-Postcard) | Gross receipts ≤ $50,000 | 15th day of 5th month after fiscal year end |
| Form 990-EZ | Gross receipts $50K–$200K | Same |
| Form 990 | Gross receipts > $200K OR assets > $500K | Same |

**XFTC (existing 501(c)(3)):**
- Verify IRS determination letter is current — check at: https://apps.irs.gov/app/eos/
- Confirm correct 990 form has been filed for each year since incorporation
- TX Franchise Tax exemption: file annual exemption confirmation with Comptroller

**PBS Foundation (pending 501(c)(3)):**
- Determine whether 1023 or 1023-EZ is appropriate (project annual revenue)
- Ensure organizing documents (articles + bylaws) contain required dissolution clause (assets go to another 501(c)(3) on dissolution)
- Obtain TX state tax exemption SEPARATELY from federal — file Form AP-204 with TX Comptroller

**Private Benefit / Inurement Rules:**
- No transactions that primarily benefit David personally through the nonprofits
- Arm's-length management services agreements between Smith Capital Holdings LLC and nonprofits are permissible — must be fair market value, documented
- No cross-entity commingling — XFTC and PBS bank accounts must be fully separate from for-profit entities

### 6. Texas Franchise Tax — Deep Dive

**Taxable Entities:** All LLCs, corporations, and other entities with TX nexus (501(c)(3)s are exempt)

**Margin Calculation (TX Tax Code § 171.101):**
Taxable margin = LEAST of:
1. 70% of total revenue
2. Total revenue minus cost of goods sold (COGS)
3. Total revenue minus compensation
4. $1,000,000

**Rates:**
- 0.75% for most businesses
- 0.375% for qualifying retailers/wholesalers
- EZ Computation: 0.331% of total revenue (for entities < $20M revenue) — simpler calculation

**No Tax Due Threshold:** If total revenue ≤ $2,470,000 (2026) — file the report but owe $0

**Late Filing Penalties:**
- 5% if filed 1–30 days late
- 10% if filed more than 30 days late
- Interest accrues on unpaid tax
- Entity loses good standing if report not filed

### 7. Texas Bar Essay Approach — Administrative/Regulatory

1. **Identify the agency and enabling statute** — what authority does the agency have?
2. **Determine the type of agency action** — rulemaking vs. adjudication vs. enforcement?
3. **Apply APA procedural requirements** — notice, hearing, record?
4. **Address judicial review** — what standard? (Substantial evidence for facts; de novo for law)
5. **Address exhaustion** — did party exhaust administrative remedies?
6. **For nonprofit issues:** Apply § 501(c)(3) operational/organizational test; address inurement; apply correct 990 form

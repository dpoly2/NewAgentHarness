## CORE COMPETENCY — Texas Bar Level Knowledge

### 1. Texas Real Property Fundamentals

**Freehold Estates:**
- **Fee Simple Absolute:** Complete ownership, freely transferable, inheritable — strongest estate. Standard for acquisitions.
- **Fee Simple Defeasible:** Subject to condition — terminates automatically (fee simple determinable) or subject to grantor's right of re-entry (fee simple subject to condition subsequent)
- **Life Estate:** Measured by a life; remainderman takes at death. Not useful for commercial acquisitions.

**Texas Homestead Law (TX Const. Art. XVI § 50):**
- Homestead protected from forced sale by creditors (except mortgage, HELOC, taxes, mechanic's liens)
- Urban homestead: up to 10 acres
- Rural homestead: up to 200 acres (family) / 100 acres (single)
- Cannot waive homestead rights except in limited constitutional exceptions
- **Relevance:** David's 1600 Spinel Rd property has homestead protections — no general creditor of his business entities can reach it

**Texas Adverse Possession (TX Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 16.024–16.028):**
- 3-year statute: adverse possessor has color of title + pays taxes
- 5-year statute: color of title + pays taxes + cultivates/uses
- 10-year statute: actual, visible, notorious, hostile, exclusive, continuous possession
- 25-year statute: with or without color of title
- **Relevance:** YEPC 110-acre site — check for any adverse possession claims; verify continuous use/cultivation history

### 2. Texas Deeds & Title

**Types of Deeds:**
- **General Warranty Deed:** Grantor warrants title against ALL claims (past and present) — best for buyer
- **Special Warranty Deed:** Grantor warrants only against claims arising DURING grantor's ownership — common in commercial transactions
- **Quitclaim Deed:** Conveys only whatever interest grantor has (may be nothing) — used to clear title clouds, NOT for acquisition
- **Deed Without Warranty:** No warranty — rare; used in foreclosures, estates

**Essential Deed Elements (TX):**
1. Written instrument
2. Grantor identified
3. Grantee identified (sufficient to identify)
4. Words of grant ("grant," "convey," "bargain and sell")
5. Legal description of property (metes and bounds, lot/block, or survey description)
6. Signed by grantor
7. Acknowledged before notary
8. Delivered and accepted

**Recording (TX Property Code § 11.001):**
- Recording provides constructive notice to all subsequent purchasers
- TX "race-notice" state: subsequent purchaser wins if (1) records first AND (2) takes without notice of prior conveyance
- **Rule:** ALWAYS record deeds immediately — same day if possible

**Title Insurance:**
- Owner's policy: protects buyer against title defects
- Lender's policy: required by virtually all lenders; protects lender only
- **YEPC acquisition:** Owner's title policy is non-negotiable — 110 acres with potential ag/utility/access complications

### 3. Purchase and Sale Agreements — Texas

**TREC Forms:** TX Real Estate Commission promulgated forms — mandatory for licensed brokers; available to parties in direct transactions

**Key PSA Provisions:**
- **Earnest Money:** Typically 1–3% of purchase price; held in escrow; releases to seller on default by buyer (liquidated damages) or to buyer on default by seller + buyer's right to sue
- **Option Period (TX):** Buyer pays option fee (negotiated, often $100–$1,000) for unrestricted right to terminate within option period (typically 7–14 days). Option fee is NON-refundable. Earnest money IS refundable if terminated during option period.
- **Financing Contingency:** Deal void if buyer cannot obtain financing within specified period
- **Inspection Contingency:** Buyer right to inspect; negotiate repairs or credits
- **Title Commitment:** Seller delivers title commitment; buyer has time to review Schedule B exceptions
- **Closing Date:** Time is of the essence in TX commercial transactions (unless expressly waived)

**YEPC-Specific PSA Considerations:**
- Survey contingency (110 acres — boundary survey mandatory)
- Environmental Phase I ESA — check for contamination, USTs, prior industrial use
- Zoning/entitlement contingency — confirm Hutto or Williamson County zoning supports YEPC use
- Utilities contingency — confirm water, sewer, electric availability to site
- Access contingency — confirm legal road access to CR 132
- TxDOT ROW — check for any pending or existing right-of-way claims along the perimeter

### 4. Commercial Leases — Texas

**Key Lease Types:**
- **Gross Lease:** Tenant pays fixed rent; landlord pays operating expenses
- **Net Lease (NNN):** Tenant pays base rent + property taxes + insurance + maintenance
- **Modified Gross:** Split expenses by negotiation
- **Percentage Lease:** Base rent + percentage of gross sales (retail)

**Critical Lease Clauses:**
- **Term and Options to Renew** — clearly define, option exercise notice period
- **Base Rent + Escalations** — fixed increases vs. CPI adjustments
- **CAM Charges** — define what's included; cap annual increases (5–10% cap common)
- **Permitted Use Clause** — broad use language protects tenant; narrow use protects landlord
- **Assignment/Sublease** — landlord consent rights; not unreasonably withheld?
- **Exclusivity Clause** — prohibits landlord from leasing to direct competitors (critical for retail/events)
- **Personal Guarantee** — landlord will demand for new LLC with no credit history; negotiate burn-off after 12–24 months of clean payment
- **Force Majeure** — COVID-era lesson: broadly drafted to include government-ordered closures

**The Elevation ATX — Event Venue Lease Issues:**
- Per-event license vs. long-term lease — license is simpler, less liability
- Verify venue has TABC authorization for alcohol service OR obtain separate BYOB exception
- Damage/security deposit terms
- Indemnification — ensure venue's liability does not flow to The Elevation ATX LLC

### 5. Texas Easements & Encumbrances

**Easement Types:**
- **Express easement:** Created by deed — most common and most defensible
- **Easement by implication:** Prior use that was apparent, continuous, reasonably necessary
- **Easement by necessity:** Landlocked parcel — must have access; TX courts recognize
- **Prescriptive easement:** Like adverse possession for access rights — 10-year continuous use
- **Utility easement:** Power, water, gas — check plat and deed records

**YEPC Site — Easement Checklist:**
- [ ] TxDOT ROW along CR 132 / SH-130 corridor — what setback applies?
- [ ] Utility easements — overhead power lines, buried gas/water lines
- [ ] Drainage easements — 110 acres likely has FEMA floodplain and drainage easements
- [ ] Agricultural easement or conservation easement — check deed chain

**Mechanic's and Materialman's Liens (TX Const. Art. XVI § 37 + TX Property Code Ch. 53):**
- Constitutional lien — attaches to property for unpaid construction/improvement work
- YEPC construction phase: Require contractor bond OR lien waivers at each payment
- Notice requirements are complex in TX — contractor must provide preliminary notice within specific deadlines
- Owner's remedy: require lien releases as condition of each progress payment
